## Commercial Risks

### Critical
- **No brand photography at launch** — Premium product at $80–$150 that cannot be visualized will not sell at that price point. No marketing channel compensates for poor or absent product imagery.
  - *Atlas mitigation:* Atlas generates a Photography Brief with shot list, mood board references, and priority sequence. Atlas flags photography as a critical path dependency in the Pre-Launch Calendar and escalates early if not initiated.

### High
- **Channel selection mismatch** — Investing launch budget in the wrong channel (e.g., Facebook/Meta at peak age demographic mismatch for 25–45 premium buyers who respond better to TikTok or email) wastes limited capital.
  - *Atlas mitigation:* Atlas runs channel evaluation with benchmarks before any budget commitment. Atlas explicitly presents expected CAC by channel at current benchmarks so the founder can make a data-informed decision.

- **Messaging fails to justify the premium** — If the copy doesn't communicate the borosilicate differentiation clearly, the $80–$150 price will appear arbitrary and conversion will be poor.
  - *Atlas mitigation:* Atlas builds messaging directly from the value-based pricing rationale (already documented in Stage 10). Atlas flags any copy that leads with product features rather than customer outcomes — the message must lead with what the customer gets, not what the product is.

### Medium
- **Influencer outreach leads to wrong-fit partnerships** — A food influencer who appeals to deal-seeking buyers will damage premium positioning even if follower counts are high.
  - *Atlas mitigation:* Atlas profiles influencers for audience demographic, engagement rate, and content quality — not just follower count. Atlas flags mismatch between influencer audience and Rise Jars' premium positioning before any commitment.

- **Pre-launch calendar not started early enough** — Press outreach has 6–8 week minimum lead times; holiday gift guide deadlines can be 4–6 months ahead. Late start means missed placement windows.
  - *Atlas mitigation:* Atlas builds the Pre-Launch Calendar with editorial deadline research built in, and flags time-sensitive activities immediately after launch date confirmation.

### Low
- **SEO content takes too long to rank** — Organic SEO takes 3–6 months minimum to produce measurable traffic. A content-first SEO strategy alone cannot drive Day 1 revenue.
  - *Atlas mitigation:* Atlas recommends SEO content as a long-term play, not a launch driver. Atlas sets accurate expectations: SEO builds over Stages 14–15; paid and social channels drive launch traffic.

## What Rise Jars Needs

Rise Jars has a premium product with confirmed pricing, a brand identity, and a marketing plan. Now it needs the operational and strategic architecture to actually sell — not just market. At $80–$150 per set with 3–5× the price of category leaders, the sales process must overcome real purchase friction. Rise Jars needs:

1. Clear channel decisions — DTC primary (Year 1), Amazon supplemental, specialty retail (Year 2) — with the margin math to show why each channel gets its priority in the sequence
2. A Sales Toolkit that can support both DTC (self-serve copy) and wholesale outreach to specialty retail buyers — these audiences need different assets
3. A competitor comparison sheet that directly addresses the most dangerous sales objection: "Why not just buy OXO or Pyrex at one-fifth the price?"
4. A realistic sales model that tells the founder what success looks like at conservative, base, and optimistic scenarios — so inventory, cash flow, and launch investment decisions are grounded in numbers
5. A post-purchase experience that turns a first buyer into an advocate — because at $80–$150, the premium experience must extend past the purchase decision all the way through first use
