## 5. Stage 9 — Intellectual Property

**Purpose:** Secure IP rights that protect the invention — filing patents, registering trademarks, structuring trade secrets — with professional legal guidance engaged and Atlas managing preparation work.

**Primary objective:** File appropriate IP protections — provisional or utility patent, trademark, trade secret documentation — with legal counsel engaged and Atlas managing prep and tracking.

**Key workflows:**
- Build the **IP Brief** — attorney-ready document compiling: invention description, Prior Art Summary from Stage 4, novel element statements, IP strategy intentions, brand name for trademark registration. Answers every question a competent IP attorney will ask in first meeting.
- Patent type decision guidance (Atlas explains plain-language trade-offs):
  - Provisional: lower cost, 12-month placeholder, establishes "patent pending" — right when design still evolving
  - Utility: full patent, up to 20 years protection, expensive/slow — right when design is stable
  - Design patent: protects ornamental appearance, not function — complementary, cost-effective, valuable for consumer goods
- Trademark registration preparation: USPTO TESS systematic search, International Class identification (Class 21: kitchen utensils), application info organized for attorney
- Trade secret identification: manufacturing processes, formulations, supplier relationships → formal documentation
- NDA template preparation for manufacturers, contractors, collaborators
- IP Filing Checklist: all pre-filing tasks with responsible party (inventor vs. attorney) and deadline
- IP Status Tracker initialization: all filed/pending assets, current status, key deadlines — living document through all remaining stages
- Critical deadline tracking: **12-month provisional-to-utility conversion window**

**Completion criteria:**
- IP attorney formally engaged
- IP Brief prepared and delivered to attorney
- At least one IP protection action initiated (provisional, utility, trademark, or formal trade secret documentation)
- IP Status Tracker reflects current filing status
- All relevant third parties (manufacturers, collaborators) have signed NDAs
- IP Filing Checklist complete

**Outputs:** IP Brief, IP Filing Checklist, Trade Secret Documentation Template, NDA Template, IP Status Tracker

**Handoff forward:** IP Status Tracker lives through all remaining stages. Trademark status feeds Stage 11 (Marketing). Patent pending status = marketing asset in Stages 11 and 14. IP Brief + attorney relationship needed for Stage 13 (Funding) due diligence.

## 6. Stage 10 — Pricing

**Purpose:** Establish a pricing strategy that reflects the product's value to the customer, fits willingness to pay, and supports a viable business model.

**Primary objective:** Produce a Pricing Strategy with recommended retail price, unit economics at target volume, gross margin analysis, and pricing rationale grounded in customer value — not just cost.

**Key workflows:**
- Three frameworks applied in sequence:
  1. **Cost-Plus floor**: Stage 7 Unit Economics Model → minimum price covering production cost + distributor/retailer margins + inventor's minimum acceptable gross margin
  2. **Competitive Benchmarking context**: Competitor pricing from Stage 2/3 → where Rise Jars' proposed price sits relative to market (context, not ceiling)
  3. **Value-Based ceiling**: Customer value in economic terms (dollars saved, frustration eliminated, outcomes improved) → maximum rational customer would pay
- Recommended price sits within floor-to-ceiling range, calibrated by competitive context and brand positioning (Stage 8)
- Unit economics cascade: revenue per unit → COGS → gross margin → channel margin → net margin
- Break-even volume: minimum units/year to cover fixed costs at chosen price
- Three pricing scenarios (lower/recommended/higher) with gross margin + break-even implications of each
- Pricing architecture decision: single price, tiered, subscription, or freemium
- Pricing Strategy Document drafting

**Completion criteria:**
- All three pricing frameworks applied
- Gross margin at recommended price meets inventor's stated minimum margin target
- Break-even analysis complete
- Pricing architecture decided and documented
- Pricing Strategy Document reviewed and confirmed by inventor

**Outputs:** Pricing Strategy Document, Unit Economics Model (Complete), Break-Even Analysis, Pricing Scenario Analysis, Pricing Recommendation

## 8. Atlas Automation Constitution — Core Principles

*Source: /docs/ATLAS_AUTOMATION_CONSTITUTION.md (Version 1.0, June 2026)*

**Core Principle: Atlas Owns Execution**
- Atlas performs the work → inventor reviews → inventor approves → inventor makes only decisions requiring human judgment
- If a feature asks the inventor to do something Atlas could do instead, the feature is not finished — it is a draft

**Principle 1 — Never Ask What Atlas Can Answer**
- Atlas researches competitors, calculates benchmarks, identifies stakeholders before asking the founder
- Every question Atlas asks must require human judgment, personal knowledge, or a value decision Atlas cannot make

**Principle 2 — Conversation Before Forms**
- Atlas prefers natural conversation over structured fields; extracts structure from conversation automatically
- Structured fields appear as confirmation of what Atlas already understood — not blank sheets to fill

**Principle 3 — Generate Before Requesting**
- Before asking founder to create anything, Atlas generates a first draft
- Applies to: patent drafts, NDAs, pitch decks, brand guides, pricing models, RFQ docs, marketing copy, financial models

**Principle 4 — Research Before Asking**
- Competitors, market size, pricing benchmarks, manufacturers, retail channels, patent landscape, regulatory requirements — Atlas researches all of these first
- Inventor provides context + judgment; Atlas provides research

**Principle 5 — Recommend Before Deciding**
- Format: "I recommend X because Y. Do you want to proceed?"
- Every decision point surfaces with: Atlas's recommendation + reasoning + trade-offs + simple confirm/redirect path

**Principle 6 — Reduce Cognitive Load**
- Before any step: Can this step disappear? Can Atlas complete it automatically? Can Atlas complete partially and surface only the judgment call?

**Constitutional requirement for Stages 8–10:**
- Stage 8: Atlas should draft Brand Positioning Statement, generate name candidates, draft Voice Guide — founder reviews/confirms, not builds from scratch
